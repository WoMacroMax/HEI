CHW Management

Community Health Workers (CHWs) play a crucial role in improving health outcomes and reducing disparities in healthcare. They act as a bridge between communities and healthcare systems, helping to connect people with the services they need. Here are some key aspects of how Health Equity Impact manages a Community Health Worker program:  

  1. Training and Certification: We provide comprehensive training programs to ensure CHWs have the necessary skills and knowledge. For example, the Washington State Department of Health offers a no-cost 10-week core competency training program. 
  1. Integration into Healthcare Systems: We  incorporate CHWs into healthcare teams to improve care coordination and patient outcomes. This can involve working with healthcare providers, insurers, and community organizations. 
  1. Sustainability and Funding: We  secure funding and resources to support CHW programs. This can include exploring reimbursement options through Medicaid, Medicare, and other insurers. 
  1. Community Engagement: We engage with the community to build trust and ensure that CHWs are effectively meeting the needs of the populations they serve. 
  1. Evaluation and Improvement: We continuously evaluate the effectiveness of CHW programs and making improvements based on feedback and outcomes.
