Field Interviewing

Health Equity Impact provides clinical field interviewing services to gather valuable data and insights from diverse populations. These services are designed to ensure that the voices of underrepresented and marginalized communities are heard and included in health research. Here are some key aspects of our clinical field interviewing services:
  • Community Engagement: We work closely with community leaders and organizations to build trust and establish strong relationships with the communities we serve. This helps to ensure that the interviews are conducted in a culturally sensitive and respectful manner.
  • Trained Interviewers: Our organization employs trained interviewers who are skilled in conducting interviews with diverse populations. These interviewers are knowledgeable about the specific health issues and challenges faced by the communities they are working with.
  • Data Collection: We use a variety of data collection methods, including face-to-face interviews, telephone interviews, and online surveys. This allows us to reach a wide range of participants and gather comprehensive data on health disparities and social determinants of health.
  • Cultural Competence: Our interviewers are trained in cultural competence, ensuring that they are aware of and sensitive to the cultural norms and values of the communities they are interviewing. This helps to create a comfortable and respectful environment for participants.
  • Ethical Considerations: We adhere to strict ethical guidelines when conducting interviews. This includes obtaining informed consent from participants, ensuring confidentiality, and protecting the privacy of the individuals being interviewed.
  • Analysis and Reporting: After the data is collected, we analyze the information to identify trends and patterns related to health disparities. We then provide detailed reports and recommendations to stakeholders, including healthcare providers, policymakers, and community organizations.
